Your privacy & how opengash works

After you log-in and select your Google Analytics websites, opengash will instruct your browser to connect to Google Analytics servers and download your data and store it in your own browser, without even passing through opengash servers. So, we never get a chance to see your data, let alone store it!

opengash uses JavaScript in your browser to rearrange the data and format it in a new, meaningful way that Google Analytics itself doesn't offer. The following image gives you a glimpse of what opengash does.

While Google Analytics groups your data per website, opengash groups your websites' data per metric, so you can quickly see how all of your websites are performing per each metric and per each period of time.

opengash is most impactful when you add several websites.

More on privacy

Keep in mind that we do store some information on our server. We store your name, your email address, link to your Google account photo, the names of the websites that you add, and the public numeric ID of each website. This information is stored so you won't have to add it every time you visit opengash.

You can delete your entire account at any time.

The source code that runs opengash is available on GitHub and is released into the public domain for anyone to review or use.
